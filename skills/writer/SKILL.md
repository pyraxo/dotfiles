# Writer

## Quick Start

When writing or editing content, follow these core principles:

1. **Write like you talk** - Use natural, conversational language
2. **Avoid AI patterns** - No delving, leveraging, or navigating complexities
3. **Be concrete** - Show, don't tell
4. **Use first person** - Share your experience, not prescriptions

## Writing Process

### Step 1: Draft

Write naturally without overthinking. If working from a transcript or voice memo, preserve the original phrasing.

See [write-like-you-talk.md](write-like-you-talk.md) for guidance on conversational writing.

### Step 3: Apply Writing Principles

Strengthen the prose using classic principles:

See [elements-of-style.md](elements-of-style.md) for:

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Using em dashes perfectly (â€”)
- Starting every sentence with "You"
- Uniform sentence length
- Excessive parallelism ("It's not X, it's Y")
- Capitalizing Everything Important
- Adding emojis unnecessarily
- Using "Moreover," "Furthermore," "Additionally"
- Hedging too much ("It may be worth considering that perhaps...")

## Key Principles Summary

1. **Concrete over abstract**: "her empty chair at dinner" not "the profound impact of loss"
2. **Active over passive**: "I remember" not "will be remembered by me"
3. **Simple over latinate**: "use" not "utilize", "help" not "facilitate"
4. **Varied rhythm**: Mix short and long sentences
5. **Committed opinions**: Take a position, don't seesaw endlessly
